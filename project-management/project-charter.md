## Business Problem

Panificadora Chask operated with outdated, improvised, and artisanal machinery that severely
limited its productive capacity and competitiveness in the local Punata and Cochabamba market.
The initial diagnostic identified the following critical problems:

- **Obsolete machinery**: egg mixing, dough kneading, and laminating were performed manually
  or semi-automatically, creating bottlenecks in the production line.
- **Excessive energy consumption**: approximately 55,000 kWh/month driven by inefficient
  electric motors older than 5 years, representing high operating costs.
- **Frequent machine failures**: high downtime disrupting the productive flow and increasing
  corrective maintenance costs.
- **Inability to meet market demand**: resulting in lost sales and deterioration of the
  company's commercial image.
- **Compromised product quality**: caused by the irregularity of manual processes and
  variability introduced by obsolete equipment.

---

## Project Objectives

1. **Energy efficiency**: reduce energy consumption by replacing electric motors and
   implementing an Energy Management System (EMS).
2. **Production capacity**: expand plant capacity to meet existing and growing market demand.
3. **Operational efficiency**: eliminate production bottlenecks by replacing artisanal
   machinery with high-performance industrial equipment.
4. **Quality improvement**: standardize production processes to deliver consistent
   product quality.
5. **Staff competency**: train personnel in the use of new machinery and preventive
   maintenance practices.

---

## Project Scope

The scope of work contracted to INGEDAV S.R.L. covers:

- Energy and operational audit of the existing plant.
- Expansion design using CAD software (SolidWorks) for new areas and equipment.
- Manufacturing, installation, and commissioning of high-performance machinery:
  industrial kneader, laminator, and mixers.
- Implementation of an Energy Management System (EMS) for continuous electricity
  consumption monitoring.
- Replacement of electric motors on equipment older than 5 years.
- Modernization of the plant's electrical system.
- Personnel training on new machinery and preventive maintenance.

**Out of scope**: sales and marketing strategy, raw material procurement, product
formulation changes.
