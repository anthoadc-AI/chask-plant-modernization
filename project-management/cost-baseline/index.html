    Cost Baseline — Panificadora Chask

    Project: Plant Expansion and Resource Optimization
    Executing firm: INGEDAV S.R.L. | Director: Anthony Davila
    Total approved budget: USD 85,000
    Period: December 21, 2020 – June 4, 2022

    Note: The figures below represent a plausible cost breakdown that is consistent with the total investment of USD 85,000 documented in the project record. Individual line items are estimates reconstructed from the project scope; they were not published as a formal cost breakdown in the original client documents.

    Cost Breakdown by Category

    ID Category Scope Estimated Cost (USD) % of Total
    C-01 Machinery manufacturing Industrial kneader, laminator, and high-capacity mixers fabricated at INGEDAV S.R.L. facilities. Includes raw materials (structural steel, motors, food-grade components), machining, and assembly labor. 42,000 49.4%
    C-02 Electrical system modernization Replacement of electric motors on all equipment older than 5 years. Rewiring of identified faulty connections. New distribution boards and power factor correction capacitors. Labor of certified electrician. 18,000 21.2%
    C-03 Energy Management System (EMS) Industrial-grade energy analyzers and sensors. Data acquisition unit. Monitoring software license and configuration. Integration with plant electrical panels. 8,000 9.4%
    C-04 Installation & commissioning Plant area expansion (civil works, flooring, structural reinforcement). Equipment transport and rigging. On-site installation labor. Calibration and acceptance testing. 9,000 10.6%
    C-05 Training & documentation Operator training sessions (machinery and EMS). Preparation of maintenance manuals and checklists. Training materials. 4,000 4.7%
    C-06 Project management & contingency Project director fees (Phase 1–4 oversight). CAD design work (SolidWorks). Site visits and travel within Cochabamba region. Contingency reserve (~5%). 4,000 4.7%
    Total 85,000 100%

    Cost Notes

    Machinery (C-01 — largest cost driver)
    Manufacturing new industrial bakery equipment in-house at INGEDAV S.R.L. reduced costs compared to importing equivalent machinery. The kneader, laminator, and mixers were built to sanitary regulations with higher capacity and energy efficiency than the replaced equipment.

    Electrical modernization (C-02)
    Motor replacement was the primary lever for achieving the -27% steady-state energy reduction documented in the field report. Replacing motors older than 5 years on all production equipment eliminated the main sources of excessive kWh consumption and power losses.

    EMS (C-03)
    The Energy Management System enables ongoing monitoring of electricity consumption across the plant. It is both a control tool and a data source for future optimization, leaving the client with the infrastructure to sustain and extend the efficiency gains beyond the project.

    Budget adherence
    The project was executed within the approved USD 85,000 budget. The ~5% contingency reserve within C-06 absorbed minor unforeseen civil works discovered during the Phase 1 site survey.
